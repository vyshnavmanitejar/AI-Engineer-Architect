### Section 2: Core Concepts & Terminology 📚
**Duration: 6-7 hours**

#### 2.1 AI vs. Related Fields
| Concept | Definition | Focus |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| **AI** | Machines mimicking human intelligence | General problem-solving |
| **ML** | Systems learning from data | Pattern recognition |
| **DL** | Neural networks with multiple layers | Complex pattern detection |
| **Augmented Intelligence** | AI assisting human decision-making | Human-AI collaboration |

#### 2.2 Types of AI by Capability
- **Narrow AI (Weak AI):** Specialized for specific tasks
  - Examples: Image recognition, chatbots, spam filters
  - Current state: All existing AI systems
  
- **General AI (Strong AI):** Can understand and perform any intellectual task
  - Status: Theoretical, actively researched
  - Estimated timeline: Unknown (5-50+ years)
  
- **Super AI (ASI):** Surpasses human intelligence in all aspects
  - Status: Purely speculative
  - Ethical considerations critical

#### 2.3 Machine Learning Paradigms
- **Supervised Learning:** Learning from labeled examples
  - Classification: Predicting categories
  - Regression: Predicting continuous values
  
- **Unsupervised Learning:** Finding patterns in unlabeled data
  - Clustering: Grouping similar items
  - Dimensionality reduction: Simplifying data
  
- **Reinforcement Learning:** Learning through trial and error
  - Agents learning from rewards/penalties
  - Examples: Game AI, robotics

#### 2.4 Deep Learning & Neural Networks
- Biological inspiration
- Layers and neurons
- Activation functions
- Backpropagation and training

## Key Takeaways

### 🎯 Core Principles
1. **AI is a broad field** with multiple subdomains and approaches
2. **Current AI is "narrow"** - specialized for specific tasks
3. **Data quality matters** - AI learns patterns from data
4. **Ethical considerations are crucial** - AI impacts society
5. **Continuous learning is essential** - AI evolves rapidly

### 💡 Important Distinctions
- Not all AI requires deep learning
- More data ≠ better predictions (quality matters)
- AI is probabilistic, not always certain
- AI augments humans, doesn't always replace them

### 🔮 Future Outlook
- Generative AI will continue transforming creative fields
- AI-human collaboration will be the norm
- Ethical frameworks will become stricter
- Regulatory bodies will catch up with technology
